Max Ingham - Portfolio Site

While it may seem strange to include the site you're currently on as a project entry here, I thought It'd be nice to have a bit of a rundown of what I wanted to achieve when creating this site. I'd always wanted a portfolio site to show off what i've been up to, but I either found myself without time, money, or both. After essentially free-lancing it for the year after my placement in London fell through, I felt this was the best time to actually rig something like this up.

-Return to Projects-

What I Did

I created the logo like usual, I must have a thing for doing that as i've either insisted on doing it or fell into doing it too many times to count, indeed, most of the projects i've ever done I have created some form of logo or branding to go with it. I knew I wanted this site to be fairly minimal in its presentation, so I went with a slightly textured, neutral background for the landing page, and sharp angled project previews for a sleeker look. As usual, I started with a HTML5 boiler plate and did it all from scratch, just the way I like it.

Why I Did It

I just wanted a centralised place to store everything relevent I'd done pertaining to my course (some of it very loosely pertaining mind you). I also kept forgetting to buy the domain name with my name in it, so after I did so when I got some cash from creating a few sites, I felt it only proper to get a site up in my name ASAP.

Who I Did It For

This one is obvious, I did it for me so I have a nicely presented portfolio of work, but also for potential employers to quickly see what i've been up to so they may build a better picture of who I am outside of my CV. A win for all!
